Beginner’s Guide to Studying Abroad

Step 1: Why You Should Study Abroad: More Than Just a Degree

Studying abroad isn’t just about earning a certificate from a foreign university. It’s a life-changing experience that can open doors in ways you might never have imagined — especially for South African students facing unique challenges and opportunities.


South Africa is rich in culture and opportunity, but studying abroad exposes you to a whole new world. It pushes you beyond what’s familiar — new cultures, new ideas, new ways of thinking. You’ll gain a global perspective that’s highly valued anywhere you go.



Research Beyond Rankings — What Fits You?

Instead of just picking the “top universities,” look deeper:

  • What kind of campus vibe do you want? Quiet or busy? Large or small?
  • What support systems do they offer international students?
  • What’s the local culture like? Can you handle the weather, food, language?
  • What are the hidden costs (healthcare, transport, books)?

Tip: Join Facebook groups or forums for current international students in that country to get real stories.

Step 2: Find a Program — How to Choose the Right Study Abroad Course for You

Now that you’ve decided you want to study abroad, the next big step is finding the right program. This is super important because it shapes your whole experience — what you learn, where you live, and even your future career. Here’s how to find the perfect fit:


Where to Find Study Abroad Programs

  • University websites — Check the official sites of universities in your preferred countries. They usually have detailed info on courses and entry requirements.
  • Study abroad portals — Websites like Studyportals or Top Universities list many programs worldwide.
  • Education fairs and advisors — Look out for local study abroad fairs or talk to registered education consultants in South Africa.
  • Scholarship websites — Sometimes scholarships are tied to specific programs, so check sites like DAAD (for Germany) or Chevening (UK).

Popular Programs for South African Students

Here are some examples to get you started:

  • UK: Bachelor’s, Master’s degrees in Business, Engineering, IT, and more. See UCAS for applying.
  • USA: Wide range of degrees and community college programs. Check Common App to apply.
  • Canada: Known for welcoming international students with degrees in Health, Tech, and Arts. See EduCanada.
  • Australia: Popular for Environmental Science, Health, and Hospitality. Visit Study in Australia.
  • Germany: Many free or low-cost courses especially in Engineering and Sciences. Check DAAD.

Step 3: Getting your documents ready early saves you stress later. Missing or wrong documents can delay your application or visa, so make sure everything is correct and certified.

Before you can pack your bags and start your adventure, there’s one super important step: getting your documents ready. Think of your documents like your passport to the future — if even one is missing or not right, your whole plan can get stuck. So, getting everything organized early means less stress, faster approvals, and one step closer to your dream of studying abroad. Trust me, a little time spent now will save you big headaches later!


Common Documents You’ll Need

  • Passport — Must be valid for the whole duration of your studies.
  • Academic transcripts and certificates — Your matric results and any other qualifications.
  • Proof of English proficiency — IELTS, TOEFL, or equivalent test results.
  • Letter of acceptance — From the university you applied to.
  • Personal statement or motivation letter — Explaining why you want to study abroad.
  • Letters of recommendation — From teachers or employers.
  • Financial documents — Bank statements or affidavits proving you can support yourself.
  • Medical reports and vaccinations — Some countries require these for visa approval.
  • Police clearance certificate — To prove you have no criminal record (if required).

Where to Find Document Requirements

  • University websites — They list exactly what documents you need for your program.
  • Embassy or consulate websites — Check visa requirements for your destination country.
  • Education consultants or advisors — They can guide you on what to prepare.
  • Official government sites — For things like police clearances or medical certificates.

Step 4: Scholarships and Bursaries for South African Students Studying Abroad

Scholarships are your golden ticket! They don’t just lighten the financial load — they prove you’re a serious, determined student ready to take on the world. Applying might seem tough, but with the right steps, you could score free money to chase your dreams. So, get ready to put your best foot forward and unlock amazing opportunities!


Chevening Scholarship (UK)

  • Fully funds a one-year master’s degree in the UK.
  • Covers tuition, living expenses, flights, and more.
  • Open to South African citizens.
  • Visit: daad.org.za

DAAD Scholarship (Germany)

  • Offers scholarships for various degree levels, including undergraduate and postgraduate studies.
  • Covers tuition, monthly stipends, travel allowances.
  • Open to South African students.
  • Visit: daad.org.za

Fulbright Scholarship (USA)

  • For postgraduate studies and research in the United States./li>
  • Covers tuition, living costs, travel, and health insurance.
  • Open to South African citizens.
  • Visit: fullbright.org.za

Commonwealth Scholarship

  • Supports students from Commonwealth countries (including South Africa) to study in the UK and other Commonwealth nations.
  • Covers tuition fees, travel, and living expenses.
  • Visit: csuk.fcdo.gov.uk"

Vanier Canada Graduate Scholarships (Canada)

  • For doctoral studies in Canada.
  • Provides generous funding for living expenses and tuition.
  • Open to international students including South Africans.
  • Visit: vanier.gc.ca

Australia Awards Scholarships

  • Fully funds studies in Australia for students from developing countries including South Africa.
  • Covers tuition, living costs, travel, and health insurance.
  • Visit: dfat.gov.au

EducationUSA Advising Centers

  • Not a scholarship but a great agency that helps South African students find scholarships and apply to U.S. universities.
  • Provides free advice and resources.

  • An organization that lists various scholarships and bursaries for South Africans going abroad.

Step 5: Visa & Travel

Your journey is almost real — the moment you’ve dreamed about is coming!

But before you can step onto that plane and into your new life, there’s an important gate to pass through: getting your student visa and preparing for travel. It can feel overwhelming, but remember—this is the final step that turns your plans into reality. Stay focused, follow the process carefully, and soon you’ll be on your way to an unforgettable adventure.


Step 1: Apply for Your Student Visa

  • Visit the official embassy or consulate website of your destination country to find visa application forms and requirements.
  • Gather all required documents: acceptance letter, passport, financial proof, medical reports, and any country-specific forms.
  • Book your visa appointment early — some embassies have long wait times.
  • Attend the visa interview, if required, and answer questions honestly and confidently.
  • Pay visa fees and track your application status online.
  • Be patient; visa processing can take weeks or months.

Step 2: Prepare for Your Travel

  • Book your flight only after your visa is approved.
  • Check luggage allowances and pack smart — include all important documents, medication, and essentials.
  • Arrange airport pickup or plan your transport from the airport to your accommodation.
  • Make copies of your passport, visa, acceptance letter, and emergency contacts — keep them separate from originals.
  • Inform your family and friends about your flight details and arrival time.
  • Learn basic greetings in the local language and read up on local customs to ease your arrival.
