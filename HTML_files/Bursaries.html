Top Bursaries & Funding Opportunities for South African Students

Finding bursaries and funding can be overwhelming, but we make it simple. Here’s a list of major bursaries, application windows, and tips to help you secure funding for your studies in 2025.

Popular Bursaries in 2025

NSFAS

Covers: Tuition, accommodation, books, and living allowances for eligible undergraduate students.
Applications Open: 01 February 2025 — Close: 30 April 2025 (Check official portal for updates).
NSFAS official website

Funza Lushaka

Covers: Full tuition, books, meals, and accommodation for future teachers.
Application window 2025: 10 Oct 2024 — 26 Jan 2025 (Next cycle updates on official site).
Funza Lushaka official site

Health Department Bursaries

Covers: Medical, nursing, and allied health programs. Students typically serve in provincial health services after graduation.
Example (Western Cape) 2025: 02 June 2025 — 30 Sep 2025.
National Dept. of Health bursary | Western Cape bursaries

Sasol Bursaries

Covers: STEM fields including engineering and sciences, with tuition, allowances, and development programs.
Application windows vary, check the Sasol bursaries portal for current cycles.

FNB / FirstRand

Covers: Commerce, science, and engineering courses with no pay-back for many awards.
Application window 2025: 01 March — 30 September 2025.
FNB bursary portal

Absa Bursaries & Fellowships

Covers: Finance, commerce, and related fields. Application windows vary per program (example: 10–19 Jan 2025).
Absa bursary/fellowship info

Standard Bank (via Study Trust)

Covers: Science, commerce, and engineering courses. Application season 2025: 01 May — 30 Sep 2025.

How to Apply for a Bursary

  1. Find bursaries matching your field of study.
  2. Check eligibility, requirements, and deadlines.
  3. Prepare your documents: ID, academic transcripts, motivational letter, proof of income.
  4. Submit applications online or as instructed by the bursary provider.
  5. Wait for responses and follow up if necessary.

Application Tips

  • Apply early to avoid last-minute issues.
  • Tailor your motivational letter for each bursary.
  • Ensure documents are clear, certified, and correctly labeled.
  • Keep copies of everything you submit.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What’s the difference between a bursary and a loan?
A bursary is funding you don’t need to repay (unless you break conditions). A loan must be repaid with interest.
Can I apply for more than one bursary?
Yes, apply to as many as you are eligible for, but typically you can only accept one offer.
Is NSFAS a bursary or loan?
NSFAS is now considered a bursary. Eligible students do not repay the funds.
